Recovering a $15M conversion drop after Vodafone's iPhone launch.

A post-launch traffic surge exposed a redirect-loop bug that quietly throttled checkout — a funnel-level diagnosis traced it to a time-out error and restored conversion within weeks.

01 — The Business Driver

Checkout conversion fell off a cliff after the iPhone launch

Basket-to-order conversion dropped from 11.0% to 9.0% following the iPhone launch and stayed depressed even after traffic volumes stabilized.

CONVERSION

Basket-to-order CVR dropped 2 points

Conversion fell from 11.0% in June to 9.0% by September, coinciding with the iPhone launch surge.

2-point CVR drop = revenue leaking at checkout
PERSISTENCE

The drop didn't recover with traffic

Conversion stayed low into October even after traffic volumes returned to normal, ruling out a simple demand spike.

Traffic recovered, conversion didn't = a product issue, not demand
SATISFACTION

Transactional NPS declined

The checkout breakdown was also dragging down post-purchase satisfaction scores.

Broken checkout = customers noticing and saying so
02 — The Obstacles

The cause was buried inside a 5-step funnel

Three questions stood between the symptom and the fix: which step was failing, why it lined up with the iPhone launch, and what in the code was actually broken.

DIAGNOSIS

No step-level visibility into the funnel

Basket → checkout → address → payment → review → thank-you conversion wasn't broken down by step, hiding where users dropped off.

No step-level data = drop-off location unknown
CORRELATION

Unclear link to the iPhone launch

The timing pointed to the launch, but nothing connected device traffic to a specific failure mode in checkout.

Timing pointed to the launch = but no causal link yet
ROOT CAUSE

A code-level bug was hidden in production

A redirect loop triggered by time-out errors was silently sending certain users into a dead end, with no alerting in place.

Silent redirect loop = orders failing with no alert
03 — The Solution

From funnel analysis to a code-level fix

A step-by-step funnel analysis surfaced a time-out/redirect-loop correlation, which IT traced to a specific code defect and shipped a fix.

1

2

3
Root cause identified by IT

4
Fix shipped and validated

Deployed the fix and confirmed conversion rates stabilized immediately, recovering through November and December.Fix deployment · validation
