01 — The Business Driver

Activation was stuck at 45% and nobody owned the fix

Recommendations are a core activation lever, but the vendor running them gave product and marketing no way to see, test, or improve them.

ACTIVATION

Two-minute watch rate was flat

Only 45% of users reached the Activation threshold of two minutes of viewing, and recommendation lanes weren't moving that number.

45% activation, flat for years = recs aren't moving the core metric
VENDOR

Ranking logic was a black box

A third-party recommender couldn't be inspected, tuned, or tied to Joyn's own catalog and editorial signals.

Ranking logic invisible = no way to test or improve it
OWNERSHIP

No product owner for recs

With nobody able to see or test ranking logic, engineering, data science, product, and marketing had no shared roadmap.

5% CTR, no owner = recs ignored by every team
02 — The Obstacles

What kept the team locked to the vendor

Cost, opacity, and a monolithic design meant every fix required a vendor ticket instead of an internal experiment.

COST

$135k/year for a closed system

The vendor contract carried a fixed annual cost regardless of whether the recommendations actually performed.

$135k/yr fixed cost = paid the same whether it worked or not
TESTING

No embedded A/B framework

Experimentation ran through Optimizely, separate from ranking, so testing new recommendation ideas was slow and disconnected.

Testing lived outside ranking = every experiment slow and disconnected
SCALE

Single-market architecture

The existing setup wasn't designed to extend cleanly to new markets like Switzerland and Austria.

Built for one market = CH/AT expansion blocked
03 — The Solution

A modular platform: candidates, ranking, presentation

The recommendation engine was rebuilt as self-contained layers so teams could test and ship independently, then extended with automated personalization and a GenAI assistant.

1
Modular engine candidates → ranking → presentation

Split the recommender into candidate generation, ranking & personalization, and presentation, letting each layer be built and tested in parallel.In-house recsys · modular architecture

2
Smart curator + cold-start lane

Reordered existing lanes by recency and user signals, and shipped a dedicated machine-curated discovery lane for new users.Presentation layer · cold-start

3
Embedded experimentation + explainability

Built A/B testing directly into the platform API to replace Optimizely, and shipped a Streamlit tool exposing ranking scores with editorial override.Streamlit · A/B framework

4
Viewing-pattern reinforcement + GenAI assistant

Detected recurring viewing habits to prioritize matching content at predicted times, and launched a conversational discovery assistant with trace-based evaluation.GenAI · pattern detection
