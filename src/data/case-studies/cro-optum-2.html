01 — The Business Driver

User journeys had to actively guide people to their health goals

Optum's digital products needed to optimize how users moved through topic pages, search, and app-download flows so the experience matched each customer's underlying objective.

CAPABILITY NEEDED

Journey-level optimization

Provider search, topic pages, and landing pages all needed structured experimentation to find layouts that moved users toward their goals.

Static layouts = users disengaging before reaching their goal
PERSONALIZATION GAP

One-size-fits-all content

Users with different category affinities (e.g. mental health, depression topics) were shown the same generic content regardless of demonstrated interest.

Same content for every user = missed chance to guide them
WHY VENDOR TOOLS FAIL

Off-the-shelf tools lack health-domain nuance

Generic CRO tooling can run tests, but prioritizing and interpreting them for sensitive health topics requires in-house strategy and analysis.

Off-the-shelf CRO tools = can't prioritize for sensitive health topics
02 — The Obstacles

Running 50+ tests a year requires a repeatable pipeline, not one-off experiments

At 50-70 tests a year, every stage — generating hypotheses, choosing which to build, and handling sensitive topics — needed its own process.

VOLUME

High test throughput

Sustaining 50-70 tests per year meant every step from hypothesis to insight needed a defined, monthly-reviewed process.

50-70 tests a year = no room for ad-hoc execution
PRIORITIZATION

Competing hypotheses across teams

Product analytics teams surfaced hypotheses faster than they could be built and shipped, requiring a prioritization layer before development.

Hypotheses outpaced build capacity = needed a prioritization step
SENSITIVITY

Sensitive topic pages

Tests on pages like "depression" topic content needed careful layout variants to find engagement gains without compromising user experience.

Depression-topic pages = layout changes needed extra care
03 — The Solution

A monthly-cadence pipeline from hypothesis to insight

A six-stage loop — recommend, hypothesis, prioritize, develop, go live, analyze — ran on a monthly review cycle, with strategy and execution owned end-to-end.

1
Hypothesis & prioritization insights → test queue

Product analytics teams identified hypotheses; strategized and prioritized which to build each month based on expected impact.Hypothesis · prioritization

2
Build & cross-team execution

Collaborated across teams to build test variants — from CTA layout changes to multivariate landing page tests (MVT).A/B · MVT

3
Targeted, affinity-based variants

Designed variants around observed category affinity, offering next-best-action content based on a user's demonstrated interests.Personalization · next-best-action

4
Go live, analyze, repeat monthly

Shipped tests, analyzed results monthly, and fed insights back into the next round of hypotheses — a continuous loop.Analysis · monthly review
