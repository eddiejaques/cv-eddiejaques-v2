Most marketing teams aren't short on data. They're short on the right level of capability.

A five-rung maturity ladder for marketing data — and why most teams are trying to climb rung 4 before they've stabilised rung 2.

Role Framework developed from 12 years working across analytics, product, and strategy roles

5 maturity levels 4 jump types: tech, collaboration, trust, infrastructure 12 years across analytics, product & strategy
01 — The Ladder

Five levels, each with its own job to finish

Each level solves a specific problem. Skipping one doesn't make a team faster — it makes every level above it unreliable.

1
Get the basics right Foundation

Fix the foundation before anything else — if it's lying to you, everything built on top of it is too.

  • Server-side tracking, so signals survive cookie blocking
  • Consent management that actually works — not a banner nobody reads
  • Clean event data landing in the warehouse with proper attribution hygiene
2
Know your audiences Solid ground

This is where most teams live. It's solid ground — it's just not the finish line.

  • First-party audience segments flowing into Braze, Meta, and Google
  • A/B testing with real holdout groups — not just creative variants
  • A next-best-action model that matches the right product to the right user
3
Know your customer's future Mindset shift

You stop optimising for the cheapest click and start optimising for the most valuable customer. Harder to build — worth every bit of the effort.

  • LTV models embedded directly into campaign bidding — not sitting in a notebook
  • Lookalike modelling built on your best customers, not your most recent ones
  • Probabilistic identity resolution stitching together web, app, and CRM signals
4
Prove marketing is working Evidence over projections

Marketing stops being a cost centre and starts walking into budget conversations with evidence. Most CMOs want to be here — fewer actually are.

  • Marketing Mix Modelling that decomposes revenue across channels, seasonality, and external factors
  • Incrementality tests running continuously, not just once a year
  • Multi-touch attribution that stops crediting the last click for everything
5
Let the machines run (some of) it Not fully here, industry-wide

The infrastructure decisions made at Levels 1–4 determine whether this level is ever realistic.

  • A semantic data layer so AI agents have something reliable to reason over
  • Agents that monitor performance, flag upstream data issues, and rebalance budgets within guardrails
  • Reinforcement learning optimising bid decisions on outcome signals, not just click-through rates

03 — What Each Jump Actually Requires

The bottleneck changes at every level

The teams that move fastest aren't the ones with the biggest budgets — they're the ones who resist the temptation to skip levels.

1 → 2

A tech problem

Tracking, consent, and clean pipelines.

2 → 3

A collaboration problem

Data science and marketing actually working together — not just handing files back and forth.

3 → 4

A trust problem

Getting finance and marketing to agree on what proof of growth actually looks like.

4 → 5

An infrastructure & patience problem

The groundwork from Levels 1–4 determines whether autonomy is ever realistic.
