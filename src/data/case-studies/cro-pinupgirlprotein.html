01 — The Business Driver

A landing page that lost visitors before they shopped

Paid search traffic for HSA-eligible enrollments hit pinupgirlprotein.com and left—bounce rates exceeded 60% before visitors ever reached the product page. The growth goal required converting that traffic into signups and purchases.

ACQUISITION

Paid search traffic wasn't converting

Visitors arriving via HSA-targeted paid search left the landing page immediately instead of moving toward enrollment or purchase.

Bounce rate >60% = paid clicks leaving before they convert
FUNNEL

Vendor templates couldn't be tuned to the funnel

Off-the-shelf landing and product page templates offered no way to isolate which page elements were driving—or killing—the move from product view to cart.

No experiment layer = page changes made on guesswork
PRIORITY

Product-to-cart was the critical drop-off

Of the full funnel—home, product, cart, checkout, orders—product-to-cart conversion was the narrowest point and the clearest lever for revenue.

Cart entry = narrowest point in the revenue funnel
02 — The Obstacles

Why the funnel stayed stuck

Each blocker pointed to the same gap: nothing on the page was tuned, or tested, for the product-to-cart decision.

ATTENTION

Value props weren't visually distinct

Key product benefits were listed as plain text, easy to skim past, with nothing drawing the eye to the value on offer before the buy decision.

Plain bullets = value props get skimmed past
MESSAGING

Hero image didn't lead with the strongest claim

The default product carousel image didn't foreground "zero grams sugar"—a differentiator likely to matter most to the HSA/health-conscious audience.

Generic hero image = strongest claim never seen
EVIDENCE

No data on what actually moves conversion

Without controlled experiments, any redesign decision was a bet—improvements (or regressions) couldn't be isolated or quantified.

No A/B data = changes can't be proven to work
03 — The Solution

Two hypotheses, run as controlled A/B tests

Run two parallel, three-week experiments on pinupgirlprotein.com targeting product-to-cart conversion: one on landing page bullet styling, one on the product detail carousel's lead image.

1
Frame the funnel Home → Product → Cart → Checkout → Orders

Map the full conversion funnel and confirm product-to-cart as the highest-leverage stage for experimentation, given the elevated bounce rate upstream. Funnel mapping · Diagnosis

2
Test colored value ticks Hypothesis 1

Replace plain bullet text with colored "tick" markers highlighting product value props, to draw visitor attention at the decision point on the landing page. A/B test · 3-week run

3
Test "Zero grams sugar" hero image Hypothesis 2

Swap the default product detail carousel image for one leading with "Zero grams sugar" messaging, and measure the impact on add-to-cart completions. A/B test · 3-week run

4
Measure lift and quantify revenue Conversion rate · Incremental revenue

Compare experiment vs. control sessions and add-to-cart completions for both tests, then translate conversion rate lift into annualized incremental revenue. Lift analysis · Revenue modeling
