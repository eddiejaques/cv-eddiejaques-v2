When I moved from Lead PM to Director, the org chart changed in a day. The way I actually worked took much longer to catch up.

As a PM, my job was to be the most informed person about my product — every metric, every edge case, every customer complaint. As a Director, that same instinct becomes a liability. If I'm still the most informed person about every product in my group, my team isn't growing.

The shift is from **owning answers** to **owning the conditions that produce good answers**: clear priorities, the right people in the room, and enough context that my PMs can make calls I'd be comfortable making myself.

A few concrete changes:

- I review fewer specs, but I spend more time on the strategy documents that shape five specs at once.
- I stopped attending most stand-ups. I started attending more skip-level 1:1s.
- My calendar moved from "what's shipping this week" to "what are we not even considering yet."

None of this means caring less about the product. It means caring about it at a different altitude.
